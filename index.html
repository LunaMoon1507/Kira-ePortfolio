03 — Cultural Reflection

Life in Seoul

What I learned culturally during my time abroad.

High-Context Communication & the Workplace
One of the most significant things I learned during my internship was how to navigate high-context communication. As an American, I’m used to direct communication and explicit instructions, but my supervisor often framed expectations as suggestions. At first, I interpreted statements like “You can do this if you want” literally. Over time, I learned to recognize the implied meaning behind those words. It showed me that effective global collaboration requires not just technical knowledge, but cultural awareness and adaptability.
Jeong (정) — Human Connection in Everyday Life
The concept of “jeong”—a deep sense of warmth and attachment between people—became easier to understand through lived experience. Strangers helped me navigate public transportation when I was lost, restaurant owners checked whether I could handle spicy food, and coworkers went out of their way to help me with the ATM. These small but consistent acts of generosity made the community-mindedness of Korean society feel very real and personal.
Han (한) — Collective Memory at the DMZ
Before our visit to the DMZ, I mentioned the trip to one of my coworkers, and our cheerful conversation instantly became somber. He reflected on his own military service near the border, making clear that the DMZ isn’t perceived as a tourist destination—it’s deeply connected to national trauma and unresolved conflict. Visiting the site myself reinforced this: the atmosphere felt heavy and reflective, and I gained a real appreciation for how historical suffering shapes a society across generations.
Collectivism & Identity in a New Context
Living in South Korea was a new experience for me as someone from culturally diverse South Florida. Being visibly different often made me more aware of my own identity—through curious stares, questions about my background, or interactions with people interested to learn about where I was from. At the same time, I admired the collectivist values around me: the orderly behavior on public transit, the respect for shared spaces, the way people followed social norms for the benefit of everyone. It contributed to a level of societal trust and cohesion I hadn’t ever experienced before.
04 — Internship Reflection

Professional Growth

Lessons I learned in a South Korean workplace.

When I arrived at GNC Solution, I brought a background in fullstack development and product thinking, but on the ground I had to rapidly learn how to apply those skills inside a Korean organizational culture. Going in, I wanted to develop my global/intercultural fluency, career management skills, and professionalism. I came out having grown in all three in ways, as well as in ways I didn’t fully anticipate.

// New Knowledge & Skills
Technically, I went deep on tools I hadn’t used before: Django on the backend, JWT authentication, RESTful API design, and a live background data-polling engine pulling from the Kraken cryptocurrency API. I also contributed to integrating an autonomous ML trading engine backed by Hermes, which reads market patterns and executes simulated trades with real-time portfolio tracking. Every week, my supervisor recommended something new for me to learn, and I made it a point to follow through on those recommendations, picking up backend and machine learning skills that directly complement my frontend experience.

// The Work Environment
GNC Solution is a small, close-knit team; that intimacy meant I was working directly under Yongchan, the CEO, alongside a fellow UF intern and a few coworkers. The company’s mission is to build useful, modern technologies that fill unique niches in people’s lives, and I got to contribute to that mission firsthand. Day-to-day, my work was largely self-directed: I was responsible for teaching myself relevant skills, setting my own milestones, and holding myself accountable to completing a real, deployable project by the end of the internship.

// Korean Workplace Culture
One of the most valuable parts of this experience was seeing how a Korean workplace balances professionalism with interpersonal relationships. Work discussions were often indirect and formal, while lunches and walks with coworkers were surprisingly direct and personal. Sharing traditional meals, talking about our families and hobbies, and simply spending time together showed me that in Korean professional culture, business is about trust, community, and mutual respect—not just productivity. I also saw how my supervisor’s support style was deeply educational: he provided videos, gave lectures, and passed down his knowledge to support my personal and professional growth.

// Lessons That Will Stay With Me
My time at GNC Solution taught me that working globally requires more than technical skills—it requires curiosity, humility, and a willingness to adapt. I returned with new hard skills in backend development and machine learning, as well as a deeper sense of how to navigate a multicultural workplace, take direction across cultural norms, and build real relationships with people from different backgrounds. These are lessons I’ll carry into every team I work with for the rest of my career.

05 — Work Portfolio

What I Built

Work products & pictures from my internship project abroad.

💻 INTERNATIONAL SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT PROJECT
AI-Powered Cryptocurrency Trading Simulator
During my internship at GNC Solution in Seoul, I collaborated with an international development team to build a full-stack cryptocurrency trading simulation platform powered by machine learning. The application uses live market data from the Kraken cryptocurrency API and allows users to manually simulate trades or delegate investment decisions to an autonomous AI trading engine. On the backend, we developed a Django-based system with RESTful APIs, JWT authentication, relational database storage, and a live data polling service that retrieves market information every 30 seconds. The machine learning component utilizes TensorFlow and LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) neural networks trained on historical cryptocurrency data to identify market patterns and execute simulated buy and sell decisions. The project provided hands-on experience with full-stack software engineering, machine learning workflows, API integration, collaborative development, and cross-cultural teamwork in a South Korean technology company.
